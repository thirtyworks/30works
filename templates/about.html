30works30days

is a month-long project for makers where participants are required to submit a new piece of work every day for the month of April. If they fail to submit something one day, they are out of the project. The project is open to everyone and any medium is encouraged. In 2021 over 571 artists of all ages are participating from 30 countries around the world!!

12ø began 30/30 in 2015 to encourage making and experimentation. With the time restriction of one day you may find that the choices you make, the corners you cut, and those ideas that you couldn’t quite realise, are just as telling as the work that you produce.

You will be able to see everyone’s work everyday throughout April on the live feed using the 30/30 icon (top left) and by following #30works30days. Each day a selection of the day's works are picked by a different special art guest and displayed on Instagram. For more information please go to the 12ø website or email us at info@thirty.works

We will be organising three workshops with Kaajel Patel, Sam Meech and Beth Bramich who will facilitate an opportunity for participants to make work, as well as to check-in and talk to each other about the work they’re making. While these events will be available to all 30/30 participants, some sessions may have limited spaces. You’ll receive an email with a link detailing the host and the event, so that you can book a place. Once we confirm your place, you will receive a link to the event on Zoom.

This year’s 30/30 has kindly been supported by Artquest, Arts Council England and all of you… and us!



30works30days will not be home to any racist, transphobic, homophobic, ableist or sexist content. We will remove any such content and reserve the right to block the user from continuing in the project. You can find 12ø’s policy and guidelines here.



FAQs

I'm out but it was because of circumstances beyond my control, can I still participate?
Just email us at info@thirty.works and we can add you back into the challenge.

I’ve just found this project, can I join?
Sorry the deadline to register for 30/30 was 29th March this year. Please subscribe to our mailing list to receive an email reminder for next year!

I need to get in touch with you? help!
No problem, if you need to get in touch please email us at info@thirty.works, please do not contact us for anything super urgent or send queries via instagram/facebook because we probably won’t see it! Email is the best way to get in touch.

When's the deadline to submit?
It's just before midnight BST (GMT+1) every day, so for 1st of April, you must submit Day 1 work by 23.59pm on 1st April!

I forgot my password. help?
Not to worry, just click ‘log in’ at the top of the page, click the ‘forgot my password’ button, use the email you signed up with, and you’ll receive an email with a link to reset your password.

I can't find my brief today!
Check your spam/junk/promotions folders. If it's not there email info@thirty.works with the subject line '30/30 help-brief'. You can also find the daily brief at the top of the SUBMIT page.

Do I have to follow the briefs?
No, the briefs are just there as a prompt if you want one.

I never got a confirmation email.
Always check your spam/junk/promotions folders for messages from info@thirty.works (feel free to add this email as a contact to avoid this).

I want to change my details.
No problem, just log in and go to ‘Edit Profile’ and there you'll be able to add and change your name, bio, website, and instagram handle.

Once I’ve submitted my work for today, can I change it?
Yes! You can edit or delete your work from the ‘My Works’ page, until the day is up. On the bottom right hand corner of the image you want to change you’ll see an edit button.

Will my work be showcased?
If you opt into sharing your work, it will be featured on the feed at thirty.works. We are having a different art guest select their favorite picks which we will post on our Instagram. We encourage participants to share works using #30works30days so you can find each other!

I don't want anyone to see my work!
You can tick the button at the bottom of the submission page to hide your work from the feed and the Instagram selection for that day. Only you (on your ‘My Works’ page) and 12ø can see what you submit.
